# Ideation: Pulse Onboarding & Certification System

Generated by `ce-ideate` (run a8c1b3cf). Subject is the **Pulse** portal (`~/Documents/GitHub/pulse`), Jera's internal HR/onboarding tool — not the `compound-engineering-plugin` repo this was run from.

## Grounding Context (Topic)

**Pulse today** — Next.js 14 + Supabase + Resend, currently frontend-first/mock-data phase. Already exists:
- Phased onboarding workflow (28 tasks, role-filtered employee/admin views)
- Policy gate (20 policies must be acknowledged before progressing)
- 5 onboarding forms with progress tracking
- Sage-University training tracker (per-junior milestones: getting_started / ilt_done / certified, with projected billable dates)
- Notification primitives (`Message`, `AdminNotification`) and employee/admin role switching

**Missing (the opportunity surface):** cert expiry/renewal fields, a central cross-firm certification registry/matrix, a manager sign-off/approval task type, scheduled/recurring notification automation, and any staffing-eligibility gate.

**External best practice (researched):**
- Role-slot task routing + in-channel nudges with direct CTA, no portal login (Enboarder); RBAC auto-provisioning on hire (Rippling); manager team-view (Workday)
- Evidence portfolios with immutable, timestamped audit trails; body-aligned CPD targets (ICAEW 20h, ACCA 40 units); Open Badge 3.0 verify-and-import of credentials
- Sage requires an **org-level certified-consultant headcount per product** to retain partner tier (e.g. "4 of 5 Sage Intacct certs, at risk in 45 days") — failure triggers tier demotion
- Healthcare credential "privileging" + airline crew "currency": the scheduling system **blocks** an un-credentialed assignment rather than warning after the fact
- Continuous evidence polling (Vanta/Drata) with green/amber/red control status
- Renewal escalation ladder 90/60/30/14/day-of, escalating to manager only after the owner goes quiet; separate the renewal-ACTION deadline from the legal expiry date

## System Shape

The seven ideas form one pipeline: **④ enablement fan-out** brings a hire in → **⑦ capability ladder** moves them to billable in stages → **② cert registry** stores what they earn → **③ renewal autopilot** keeps it valid → **① deployable-state gate** governs who can face a client → **⑤ partner-tier cockpit** rolls it up commercially → **⑥ commercial-asset generator** turns the dataset into proposals and audit packs.

## Ranked Ideas

### 1. Deployable-State Engine + Staffing Gate
**Description:** Pulse continuously derives each consultant's live "deployable to client" status from cert validity + recency-of-practice + onboarding completion. At the staffing moment it returns green (assign), amber (supervised only), or red (blocked) for a given Sage product line — modeled on healthcare privileging and airline crew currency (block the assignment, don't just warn). Graded, not strictly binary (insurance risk-class model).
**Rationale:** The most expensive failure is staffing an under-certified or stale consultant on a client engagement. This moves the check to the moment money and reputation are committed, and is the single biggest leap beyond a passive HR tracker.
**Downsides:** Requires integration with the staffing system (Zoho Projects). Full hard-block is a larger build; a graded advisory version is the pragmatic first step.
**Confidence:** 80%
**Complexity:** High
**Status:** Unexplored

### 2. Self-Maintaining Certification Registry
**Description:** One central store of every consultant's certifications, each with an expiry date and an attached, timestamped, tamper-evident proof document (audit-ready). Avoids manual data entry where possible: where Sage/Credly issue digital badges, the consultant pastes a link and Pulse reads the credential name + expiry automatically (Open Badge verify-and-import). The registry/matrix is derived from those events rather than hand-maintained.
**Rationale:** Directly answers the "central place to upload and track validity" need. Hand-typed registries rot (one wrong date misfires renewal alerts); verified import makes it accurate by construction.
**Downsides:** Auto-import only works where the issuer supports digital badges; manual upload remains the fallback. Need to confirm which Sage certs issue Credly/Open badges.
**Confidence:** 88%
**Complexity:** Medium
**Status:** Unexplored

### 3. Self-Driving Renewal Autopilot
**Description:** Two dates per cert — the legal expiry and the earlier "must start renewal by" action deadline (exam slots book weeks out). Reminders fire against the action deadline on a 90/60/30/14-day ladder, escalating to the manager only if the owner goes quiet, and (where possible) pre-loading the Sage re-cert link / requesting budget approval rather than only nagging.
**Rationale:** Directly answers "notify them when they need to renew," but kills the "knew it expired but the exam was 8 weeks out" trap and keeps the owner out of the chase-up loop.
**Downsides:** Notification fatigue if cadence isn't tuned; needs an email/Teams channel wired up.
**Confidence:** 90%
**Complexity:** Medium
**Status:** Unexplored

### 4. Zero-Touch Enablement Fan-Out
**Description:** A confirmed hire fires one event that fans out enablement tasks to role slots (IT, manager, buddy, finance, Sage portal seat), with nudges delivered in Teams with one-click actions (no portal login required). A "who's blocking me?" view names the live bottleneck per hire; a cohort heatmap surfaces systemic stalls across a hiring wave.
**Rationale:** Directly answers "notifications to each representative to enable the new hire." In rapid hiring the bottleneck is coordination, not work; meeting stakeholders in-channel is how Enboarder/Rippling drive completion rates.
**Downsides:** Teams integration is real work; role slots must be defined once.
**Confidence:** 85%
**Complexity:** Medium-High
**Status:** Unexplored

### 5. Partner-Tier Certified-Headcount Cockpit
**Description:** Rolls individual certs up to the org constraint that matters: "Jera holds 4 of the 5 certified Sage Intacct consultants needed for partner tier — at risk in 45 days," flagging which renewal or new cert tips the firm over the threshold.
**Rationale:** Losing partner tier hits lead-sharing, margins, and co-marketing. No off-the-shelf tool watches this — a gap Pulse can own. This is the manager/owner view that actually moves money.
**Downsides:** Requires the exact Sage tier rules encoded, and they change over time.
**Confidence:** 82%
**Complexity:** Medium
**Status:** Unexplored

### 6. Certs as a Commercial Asset
**Description:** The same registry auto-generates the "certified team" appendix for client proposals and the partner-audit evidence pack, straight from records already kept for compliance.
**Rationale:** These are produced by hand under deadline today. Captured once for compliance, the data also wins deals and passes audits — classic compounding.
**Downsides:** Lower priority than 1-4; depends on idea 2 being solid first.
**Confidence:** 75%
**Complexity:** Medium
**Status:** Unexplored

### 7. Graduated Capability Ladder
**Description:** Replace binary "certified / not" with rungs (apprentice → associate → certified → specialist) per Sage module, each with an appropriate billing rate, extending the existing training tracker. Onboarding never fully "ends" — it becomes a rolling capability record across the consultant's tenure as Sage ships new versions.
**Rationale:** Real Sage mastery takes months across GL/AR/AP/Consolidations/Payroll. A binary gate either benches capable juniors or over-promises clients; rungs let juniors bill at the right rate immediately.
**Downsides:** Defining the rungs and rates is a one-time policy exercise the owner must lead.
**Confidence:** 80%
**Complexity:** Medium
**Status:** Unexplored
